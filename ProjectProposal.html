The impact of COVID-19 on Hate Crimes in Canada

Lab 8 - Final Project Proposal

Systemic racism is a major problem in Canada. After 9/11, there was a growing sentiment of hate towards Muslim communities, which is known as islamophobia. Now, Asian communities are particularly vulnerable due to Covid-19 racism and xenophobia, enhancing hate crimes all over the country.

The FBI has defined a hate crime as a “criminal offence against a person or property motivated in whole or in part by an offender’s bias against a race, religion, disability, sexual orientation, ethnicity, gender, or gender identity” (FBI, 2022).

Section 319 of the Criminal Code in the Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms states that inciting or promoting hatred is a criminal offence, and the maximum penalty is two years of imprisonment.

Statistics Canada shows that police-reported hate crimes almost double in Canada from 2016 to 2020 with 1,409 and 2,669 respectively.

The goal of this project is to raise awareness about this issue by creating visual representations such as graphs and maps and identifying racism hotspots in Canada. An example of a hotspot is the city of Vancouver because hate crimes increased by 39% at the beginning of the pandemic with 378 police-reported hate crimes in 2020 (Statistics Canada, 2022). Indeed, Vancouver gained the title of 'anti-Asian hate crime capital of North America' (The Guardian, 2021).
